The Portfolio Story This Period

VRLBI Hit the April 711 Conversion Goal Three Days Early

VRLBI delivered 450.32 conversions in this period alone, taking April month-to-date to 737.91 conversions against the 711 target. Goal achieved at 103.8% with two days of April still to run. PMax was the exceptional driver: $0.85 CPA (was $1.25), with conversions up 41.9% on a 2.7% reduction in spend. The Generic budget at 97% utilisation has clearly become the volume bottleneck. Budget Lost IS deepened to 65.37% as auction demand grew faster than the $22.50 daily cap could absorb.

VROCNJ Recovered Sharply From the Prior Period Crisis

VROCNJ CPA improved from $9.76 to $6.35 (-35%). Generic Last Minute, including the new DSA ad groups, dropped from $14.49 to $7.92 CPA (-45.3%) with conversions up 78.9% on a 2.2% reduction in spend. CVR climbed from 8.06% to 11.13%. The diagnosed issue from the last deck has been resolved through the DSA launch, bid recalibration, and applied negatives. Generic Budget Lost IS collapsed from 39.60% to 1.70%, while Rank Lost IS rose to 84.15% as the constraint rotated from budget to quality. PMax is now flagged "limited by budget" at $5.53 CPA, signalling clear scaling headroom.

VRWW Sits on the Target Line - Diagnosis Confirmed as Inventory, Not Funnel

VRWW CPA rose from $4.38 to $6.95, just under the $7.00 target. Both campaigns saw CVR drop in lockstep (PMax 13.45% to 8.27%, Generic 10.34% to 8.04%) despite our Search IS rising to 24.54% and Generic Rank Lost IS slightly improving. After the funnel investigation last week, Duane confirmed via the Wildwood availability snapshot that high-demand weeks of 7/11 to 8/8 are sitting at 77-81% booked. Wildwood is also the most price-sensitive market in the portfolio. The CVR pattern is consistent with traffic landing on inventory that no longer matches their search dates or budget. This reframes the response: the action is calendar-aware bidding and ad copy adjustments rather than a landing page audit.
