Key Strategic Findings

PMax Outperforms Generic in Two of Three Accounts

A significant reversal from the Q1 plan. VRLBI PMax delivers $0.78 CPA vs Generic $1.20. VROCNJ PMax delivers $5.71 vs Generic $5.75. Q2 2026 Strategy and Roadmap

Q1 validated the portfolio's efficiency and scaling potential. Q2 enters the peak booking and pre-summer period. The strategy focuses on converting budget headroom into conversion volume while maintaining CPA discipline.

1. Budget Scaling Plan

VRLBI: Aggressive Scale

  • Generic is losing 69.51% IS to budget at only 2.28% lost to rank. This is the clearest scaling signal in the portfolio. Increase Generic daily budget from $18 to $25 in 20% increments with 48-72 hour stabilization between steps.
  • PMax is the efficiency leader at $0.78 CPA. Increase PMax daily budget from $15 to $20. Monitor for learning phase disruption.
  • Target Q2 monthly spend: $1,200-$1,500/month (up from ~$988/month in Q1). Monitor the 15% CPA threshold at each increment.

VROCNJ: Priority Scale

  • Generic is losing 74.05% IS to budget. This is the single biggest scaling opportunity in the portfolio. Increase daily budget from $15 to $22 using 20% increments.
  • PMax at $5.71 CPA is performing well after reactivation. Hold PMax at $20/day and let performance stabilize before scaling.
  • Conversion rate improved 33.8% QoQ. The account is earning the right to more budget. Target Q2 monthly spend: $1,000-$1,200/month.

VRWW: Stabilize, Then Scale

  • March CPA trend ($3.62 to $4.95) needs to stabilize before adding budget. Hold current budgets through April and monitor CPA direction.
  • If April CPA holds under $5.00, increase Generic from $25 to $30/day. If CPA continues rising, pause scaling and investigate root causes.
  • Generic lost IS to budget doubled to 22.76%. The headroom exists once CPA stabilizes.
  • PMax "bidding strategy constrained" flag warrants investigation. Review target CPA settings and consider bid strategy adjustment.

2. Search Term Hygiene

  • Competitor brand terms: VRBO search terms are spending $48.54 on VRLBI alone. Add "vrbo," "airbnb," "booking.com," and "hometogo" as negative keywords across all Generic campaigns. These searches indicate platform loyalty, not property research.
  • "By owner" terms: Spending $70+ across the portfolio (VRLBI $33.98, VRWW $36.28). These users want direct owner contact, not a listing platform. Add "by owner," "owner direct," "for sale by owner" as phrase match negatives.
  • Realtor and real estate terms: Low spend but off-intent. Add "realtor," "realty," "real estate," "mls," "for sale" as negatives.
  • Under-25 rentals (VRWW): "Wildwood house rentals for 18 year olds" appeared. Per Duane's guidance, add "18 year old," "prom," "senior week," "under 21," "under 25" as negatives on VRWW.
  • Pet-friendly terms: Active across VRLBI ($10.07) and VRWW ($0.34). Still pending client confirmation on pet-friendly property availability. Hold negatives until confirmed.

3. Device Strategy by Account

  • VRLBI: Mobile ($0.88) and Desktop ($1.05) both strong. Maintain balanced bids. No changes needed.
  • VRWW: Device flip strategy delivered. Gap closed from 127% to 2.4%. Continue monitoring. If gap remains narrow, reduce bid adjustments to neutral.
  • VROCNJ: Mobile ($4.73) significantly outperforms Desktop ($7.33). Consider increasing mobile bid adjustment +15% and reducing desktop -10% to shift spend toward the more efficient device.

4. Campaign Structure Optimization

  • VRLBI Generic optimization score at 58.9%. Review Google's recommendations. The low score alongside strong performance suggests optional recommendations (like broad match expansion) that may not align with current strategy. Evaluate each recommendation individually.
  • VRWW PMax "bidding strategy constrained" status. This flag indicates the campaign cannot achieve the target CPA within its current configuration. Review the target CPA setting and consider whether the $20/day budget is sufficient for the bid strategy to operate effectively.
  • VROCNJ PMax reactivation monitoring. PMax has been running for one full quarter since reactivation. Performance is strong at $5.71 CPA. Continue monitoring for any learning phase fluctuations in Q2.

5. Upcoming Strategic Initiatives

  • ZIP code geo-targeting rollout: Implement bid adjustments based on the 104K+ customer interaction heatmap. Philadelphia DMA (~35% of leads) gets +25% bid modifier. NYC metro (~19%) gets +30%.
  • Customer match remarketing: Setup using the client's email database for Q2 campaigns. Target past inquiries and previous guests with tailored messaging for 2026 bookings.
  • Berger Realty competitor campaign (VROCNJ): Confirmed active in auction insights. Proposal remains an open item. With VROCNJ now stable and under target, Q2 is the right time to evaluate this initiative.
  • 2026 ad copy refresh: Update all ad copy to reference summer 2026 availability, early booking incentives, and seasonal messaging as peak season approaches.
  • Conversion rate optimization: VRWW conversion rate (14.80%) and VROCNJ (13.85%) are strong but below VRLBI (30.42%). Investigate landing page differences to identify improvement opportunities.

Efficiency Guardrails

Scaling Rules (Unchanged from Q1)
  • 20% increment rule: Budget increases in 20% steps with 48-72 hour stabilization periods between each step. No 3-4x jumps.
  • 15% CPA threshold: If any budget increase causes CPA to rise more than 15% from the pre-increase baseline, pause the increase and investigate before proceeding.
  • Root cause first: When CPA spikes, diagnose before pulling levers. Rank loss, quality score, learning phase disruption, and device-level inefficiency are common causes. Budget pullback is often the wrong response.
  • Budget reallocation: Per Duane's guidance, if efficiencies are not achieved in a month and budget is not used up, reallocate to future months where better efficiency is seen.

Items Requiring Client Input

  • Pet-friendly property confirmation: Pet-friendly search terms are active across VRLBI and VRWW. If the client confirms pet-friendly properties are available, these terms should remain. If not, add as negatives to prevent wasted spend.
  • Wildwood renter age policy: Confirmation from Whitney on the minimum renter age for Wildwood properties. This determines negative keyword scope for under-25 search terms.
  • Berger Realty competitor campaign approval: The proposal has been an open item since Q4. VROCNJ is now stable enough to support this initiative. Need go/no-go decision.
  • Customer match email database access: Need the email file to build remarketing audiences for Q2 campaigns.
  • Q2 budget confirmation: Q1 actual spend was $9,396 against the $33,900 Q1 budget ceiling. Confirm Q2 budget parameters and any adjustments to the "max spend, not must spend" framework.

Summary

Q1 2026 delivered the strongest portfolio performance to date. All three accounts finished under their individual CPA targets for the second consecutive quarter, with a blended portfolio CPA of $2.10 against a $3.00 target. Total conversions grew 51.7% while CPA improved 3.4%.

VRLBI remains the anchor at $0.95 CPA with 3,105 conversions. PMax emerged as the clear performance leader at $0.78 CPA. The primary growth constraint across the portfolio is budget, not rank. VRLBI Generic is losing 69.51% IS to budget. VROCNJ Generic is losing 74.05%. These are accounts with strong ad quality that are purely budget-constrained. The Q2 roadmap focuses on unlocking this headroom through phased budget increases while maintaining the established efficiency guardrails.
