The biggest constraint right now

Budget, not performance

The engines are proven under target. We are capping them at the budget line just as peak demand arrives.

Every account is comfortably inside its CPA target, yet portfolio spend fell 4% in May and large blocks of impression share are being surrendered to budget. The bottleneck is no longer rank, copy, or bidding. It is how much budget we deploy into the highest-intent window of the year. June is the last big-budget month before the planned July and August step-downs on LBI and Wildwood, so the deployment decision is now.

Rank solved, budget now the ceiling

The ad strength fix did exactly what it was meant to. Lost IS to rank on Generic sits at 7.73% and PMax rank pressure eased from 87.25% to 81.86%. PMax remains the engine at a $0.78 CPA with conversions up 10.9%. The apparent conversion-rate dip on Generic is the attribution lag, not a funnel problem: clicks landed instantly, up 15.6%, while conversions are still maturing.

What we learned

With rank fixed, the only thing holding LBI back is budget. Generic still surrenders 61.85% of impression share to budget at a CPA 36% under target. This is the single largest unrealised opportunity in the portfolio and the clearest place to push in June.

PMax carries, Generic is the soft spot

The calendar-aware bidding install is compounding. PMax delivered 9.1% more conversions on flat spend at a $4.55 CPA and now drives 73% of the account. The back half of May ran 71% more conversions than the first half, a strong shape into June. The drag is Generic: conversions roughly halved, conv rate fell to 6.29% and CPA at $6.51 is the only campaign over its target, now flagged search-volume and bidding limited.

What we learned

A clear Generic win, with PMax mid-reset

Generic Last Minute, including the DSA ad groups, is the standout: the budget cap cleared completely, CPA fell 26% to $6.35 and conversions rose 10.5% on 18% less spend. The offsetting move to flag is PMax, down 20.4% in conversions at a $7.14 CPA on 6.7% more spend. That is the late-May asset-refresh recalibration window plus attribution lag, but the swing is real and it pulled the blended CPA up. Computers were the weak device at a $9.69 CPA, near the target ceiling.

What we learned

PMax holds 83.96% of impression share back behind budget, so the headroom for the agreed June step-up is enormous, but it only unlocks once the refresh stabilises. OCNJ is the account to push hardest in June, and the one where Demand Gen reach matters most given the computer-side softness.
