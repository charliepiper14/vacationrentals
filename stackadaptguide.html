A · The short version

Why StackAdapt, in one minute

StackAdapt is a programmatic advertising platform. It lets us place your video ads inside streaming TV apps, and place mobile ads on the phones of people who are physically at the shore, then quietly add everyone we reach into an audience we can market to again later.

This summer flight has one job. While families are at the Jersey Shore, get the Vacation Rentals brand in front of them on the big screen and on their phones, and build a large warm audience from everyone we reach. That audience then feeds the booking push that runs from January to July next year, which is when most of the bookings actually happen.

Because of that, success here is measured on how many of the right people we reach and how big the audience pool grows, not on instant bookings during the summer. People do not book next summer's rental while they are sitting on this summer's beach. We are planting, not harvesting.

D · Tracking foundation

The pixel, locate or create

The pixel is one small piece of code on your website. It does two jobs for us. It lets StackAdapt recognise people who visit your site so we can build and grow the audience pool, and it lets us see when an ad led to a site visit or an inquiry. Without it, the campaigns still run, but we lose the audience building and the measurement, which are the whole point of this flight.

First, check whether one already exists

You have run programmatic before, so a StackAdapt pixel may already be on the site. Five minutes here can save a duplicate setup.

  1. Look in StackAdapt.In the left menu open the Pixels page. If a Universal Pixel is listed, it already exists. Note its ID, you are done creating and can skip to installing or verifying.
  2. Look in Google Tag Manager.Open your GTM container and scan the tag list for anything named StackAdapt. If it is there and published, the pixel may already be live.
  3. Ask the previous provider.If a past agency set up StackAdapt, ask whether an account and pixel exist under the business so we can take them over rather than start fresh.

If none exists, create the Universal Pixel

We can do this with you

This is the one technical step, and we will sit on the call for it

If you would rather, hand this section to whoever manages the website and we will guide them. It is a copy and paste, not a code change to your custom system.

  1. Open the Pixels page.In StackAdapt, left menu, Pixels.
  2. Install the pixel.Click Install StackAdapt Pixel. A code snippet appears that contains your Universal Pixel ID. This single Universal Pixel covers the whole site and all three campaigns.
  3. Copy the snippet.Copy the full snippet to hand to whoever installs it, or to paste into Google Tag Manager in the next step.

Install it through Google Tag Manager, the easy route

You already use Google Tag Manager, which means the pixel can go live across every page of your custom site without touching the site's code. This is the recommended path.

  1. Open your GTM container.Go to tagmanager.google.com and open the Vacation Rentals container.
  2. Create a new tag.Tags, then New, then click Tag Configuration and choose Custom HTML.
  3. Paste the snippet.Paste the StackAdapt Universal Pixel snippet into the HTML box.
  4. Fire it on every page.Click Triggering and choose All Pages, so the pixel loads everywhere.
  5. Name and save.Name it StackAdapt Universal Pixel and Save.
  6. Publish.Click Submit, then Publish, to push it live. The pixel is now active site wide.
Alternative

Direct install, if you prefer not to use GTM

Your web developer can paste the same snippet into the site template just before the closing head tag, so it loads on every page. Either route works. GTM is simply faster and needs no developer.

E · Measurement

Conversion tracking

On top of the site wide pixel, we set up one conversion event so we can see when an ad led to a real rental inquiry. We point it at the same inquiry action you already count as a conversion in Google Ads, so the two platforms stay consistent.

  1. Start a new event.On the StackAdapt Pixels page, click Create New, then Conversion Event.
  2. Set it as a website page load.Choose Website as the install location and Page Load as the activation method.
  3. Name it and categorise it.Name it something clear like Rental Inquiry and pick the closest conversion category, such as a lead or form submission.
  4. Copy the unique ID.Create the event and copy its Conversion Event Unique ID.
  5. Fire it on the confirmation page.We attach that ID to the inquiry confirmation or thank you page, either with a URL rule in StackAdapt or a small tag in GTM, so it counts only when an inquiry actually completes.
Expert note

We will keep view through and click through both on

People do not click an ad on a television. StackAdapt's default counts both view through and click through, which is exactly what we want, so a household that sees the streaming ad and later visits the site still gets credited. We confirm this is left on for every event.

F · The plan

What we will build

For each of the three markets, two coordinated tactics, weighted a little more heavily toward Wildwood and Ocean City where the audience opportunity is larger. You do not need to action anything here, it is for visibility.

Connected TV, the streaming screen

  • Your 30 second and 15 second videos served inside streaming TV apps on smart TVs in households inside the target zip codes
  • Built from the 1080p 16 by 9 masters already produced. StackAdapt transcodes them to fit each streaming app, so one upload covers all of it
  • Frequency held to roughly three to four views per household per week, enough to be remembered, not enough to annoy

Mobile geofencing, the on the ground screen

  • Ads served to phones physically present at beaches, boardwalks, attractions, rental clusters and the bridges into each town
  • The bridges matter, one causeway into LBI, three into Wildwood, four into Ocean City. Fencing the spans and approach roads tags arriving families into the audience as they drive in
  • Everyone reached this way is added to the pool we remarket to through the booking season

Frequency, set by weekly turnover, not by day

The cap is weekly rather than daily on purpose. Rental homes turn over to a fresh family most Saturdays, so across an eight week summer the true number of different families we reach is far larger than the number of homes suggests. A weekly cap keeps a steady presence with each family without wasting impressions.

How we will judge it

Reach and unique households, frequency, video completion rate, growth of the retargeting audience, and view through site visits from people we showed the ads to. These are the right measures for an audience building flight. The booking return shows up later, in the season these audiences are built for.

H · What happens next

After launch

The first stretch is a settling in period while the platform learns where your audience is and how to deliver efficiently. We watch it closely and adjust.

  • We monitor reach, frequency and pool growth weekly and tune targeting and pacing as the data comes in
  • If budget ever tightens, we trim frequency rather than cut weeks, to protect the number of different families reached
  • The audience we build all summer becomes the retargeting backbone for the January to July booking push, alongside the YouTube audience and your guest database
  • You get clear updates the same way you do now, so you always know what is reaching whom and what it is building toward
