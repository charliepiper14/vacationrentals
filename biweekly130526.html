The Portfolio Story This Period

Portfolio-Wide CVR Softening, Same Auction Conditions, Same Volume

All three accounts saw conversion rate drop in the 40 to 55 percent relative range this period, while spend, clicks, and cost per click stayed broadly flat or improved. VRLBI Generic CVR went from 35.13% to 18.17%, VROCNJ Generic from 12.38% to 6.86%, VRWW Generic from 8.57% to 6.72%. PMax dropped on the same pattern in all three accounts. This is the strongest synchronised CVR signal we have seen across the portfolio in this engagement. The auction is unchanged. Volume is unchanged. Quality of the click matched against what is available is the variable.

Auction Position and Costs Improved Across the Board

Cost per click dropped 15.6% on VRWW Generic, 19.2% on VROCNJ Generic, and held at $0.42 on VRLBI Generic. Impression share rose on both VRLBI Generic (26.89% to 27.47%) and VROCNJ Generic (13.93% to 14.27%). Top of page rates held above 90% across all three accounts. The platform is rewarding our quality with cheaper auctions, and budget caps continued to bind on the two accounts where we want scale (VRLBI Generic at 99.7% utilisation, VROCNJ PMax at 98.5%). The infrastructure is healthy. The conversion rate is the variable to fix.

Plan for the Next Two Weeks: Asset Health Before Budget Scale

Last week the plan was to scale VRLBI Generic from $22.50 to $27 and VROCNJ PMax from $20 to $24. Pushing more budget into a 40 to 55 percent CVR drop locks in worse CPA. The correct sequence is reversed for this fortnight: refresh PMax assets and Generic ad strength on all three accounts, apply the negatives that have been outstanding, and hold daily caps where they are. When CVR signal recovers above prior levels we step into the scaling plan from a stronger foundation. Pre-Memorial Day prep for the high-volume booking window starting late May into June.
